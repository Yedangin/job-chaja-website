Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 15, 2028

Yedangin Co., Ltd. (“Company” or “we”) operates the Jabchaja recruitment platform, which connects individual job seekers with business recruiters. We are committed to protecting your personal information in accordance with applicable laws and global best practices. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect from users of Jabchaja, including individual users (job seekers) and business users (employers/recruiters), and how we use, share, and safeguard that information. It also outlines the distinctions in data handling for individual vs. business users, our outsourcing arrangements, international data transfers, and your rights regarding your personal data.

ℹ️ By using the Jabchaja platform, you agree to the collection and use of your information as described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, you should refrain from using our services. We do not knowingly allow persons under the age of 15 to register as job seekers in compliance with labor and privacy laws.

1. Information We Collect

  • •We only collect personal information that is necessary to provide our recruitment services. The information collected may differ for individual job seekers and business recruiter accounts, as described below.

2. For Individual Job Seekers

  • •Account Information: When you register as an individual user, we collect information such as your name, email address, phone number, date of birth, login credentials, and other contact details. This is used for account creation, verification of identity, and communication.
  • •Profile and Resume Data: You may provide personal details on your profile or resume, including education history, work experience, skills, certifications, references, and any other information typically found in a CV. This can also include a photograph or professional profile image (if you choose to upload one).
  • •Job Application Information: When you apply to a job through Jabchaja, we collect the application details such as your resume/CV, cover letter, application answers, and any messages or correspondence related to your application. These details are transmitted to the business (employer) you choose to apply to.
  • •Usage Data: We collect certain information automatically when you use the platform. This may include your IP address, device type, browser type, access times, pages viewed, and referral links. We may also use cookies or similar technologies to collect usage analytics and to remember your preferences.
  • •Optional Information: If you opt in to additional services, we may collect information such as your location (for location-based job searches), or marketing preferences (e.g. your consent to receive newsletters or job alerts via email or SMS). Providing this information is optional and only collected with your consent.
  • •Note: Please avoid submitting any sensitive personal information (e.g. social security numbers, financial information, health information, or details about race, religion, or political opinions) on your resume or profile, unless it is specifically requested as per applicable law or necessary for a particular job application. Jabchaja does not require such sensitive data for general use of the platform.

3. Data Retention and Deletion

  • •We retain personal information only for as long as it is necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law. The retention period can vary depending on the type of information and the user’s actions
  • •Job Posting Details: When business users create job postings on Jabchaja, we collect the content of the job listing. This includes job descriptions, qualifications, salary range, work location, and any contact information the recruiter chooses to provide for applicants (such as a point of contact’s name, phone, or email if included in the job description). Job posting information is generally considered business information, but it may incidentally include personal data (for instance, if a personal work email or direct phone number of a recruiter is used).
  • •Active Account Data: Information associated with your account (both individual and business accounts) is kept for as long as your account remains active. You can access and update much of your basic account information by logging into your profile. If you decide to delete your account or request deletion, we will remove or anonymize your personal information from our active databases, except for data we are required or permitted to retain by law (see below). When an account is terminated (either by your request or due to inactivity or violation of terms), your data will no longer be visible on the platform to others (e.g., your profile and job postings will be taken down). We aim to process account deletion requests promptly.
  • •Job Applications: If you are a job seeker and you applied to a job, the content of that application (your resume, etc.) has been shared with the employer. We do not control how long the employer retains that information; they may retain it as part of their own recruitment records. Within our platform, we may retain a record of the fact that you applied (e.g., in your application history) for a certain period, but this is typically only accessible to you and not to others.
  • •Legal Retention Requirements: Certain laws require us to retain specific information for fixed periods. For example:
  • •Records of electronic communications with you (for example, customer service inquiries or contract-related communications) may be retained for 3 years under consumer protection laws.
  • •Web log data recording visits or service use may be retained for a minimum period (such as 3 months) if required by telecommunications laws in some jurisdictions.
  • •If any dispute arises or if it’s necessary to enforce our agreements, we may retain relevant information until the issue is resolved. In general, even after you delete your account, we might retain information necessary for compliance with legal obligations, such as preventing fraud or resolving disputes. This retained data will be securely stored and isolated from routine use.
  • •Deletion and Disposal: When the retention period has been met, or if you request erasure of your data and we have no lawful basis to keep it, we will permanently delete or irreversibly anonymize your personal information in a secure manner. Physical records will be shredded or incinerated, and electronic files will be deleted using secure methods that prevent them from being recovered or reconstructed. Before disposal, we ensure that personal information cannot be accidentally disclosed or leaked.
  • •Backup and Residual Copies: Please note that due to the nature of backups and caching, your data might persist in our secure backup storage for a short period after deletion (typically until the backups are overwritten or destroyed on a regular cycle). During this period, we will maintain the security and confidentiality of those backups and will not use the information except if necessary for disaster recovery or legal purposes.
  • •Once the retention period expires or data is deleted upon user request, the personal information is inaccessible to anyone except as required by law. If you have any specific questions about our data retention practices, you may contact us using the information in the Contact section.

4. Purposes of Use of Personal Information

  • •We use the collected personal information for the following purposes, separated by user type where applicable. We ensure that all use of personal data is lawful and in accordance with the consent you have provided and applicable regulations
  • •Providing and Operating the Service: We use personal information to create and manage user accounts, authenticate users at login, and enable core platform features. For job seekers, this includes maintaining your profile and resume and allowing you to search and apply for jobs. For recruiters, this includes posting job vacancies, searching the candidate database (if such feature is available with appropriate consent from job seekers), and managing applications received.
  • •Facilitating Recruitment Processes: Personal information is used to connect job seekers with employers. For example, if you are a job seeker, the details on your profile and any application you submit will be forwarded to the recruiter of the job you applied for. Recruiters will use this information to evaluate your candidacy and to contact you. Conversely, certain business contact details (like company name and location, and possibly the recruiter’s name or email) are displayed to job seekers for transparency and communication in the hiring process.
  • •Communication: We use contact information (email address, phone number) to send important notices to users. This includes service-related announcements such as updates to our terms or privacy policy, notifications about job applications (e.g., confirmations that an application was submitted or viewed), interview requests or messages from recruiters to job seekers (relayed through our platform), and responses to support inquiries. If you contact us for customer service or technical support, we will use your provided information to assist you.
  • •Personalization and Improved User Experience: We may use information about your usage and preferences to personalize the job recommendations or content you see on Jabchaja. For instance, we might suggest jobs to individual users based on their profile, search history, or location. We also analyze how users interact with the platform to improve our interface, features, and services. This may include using analytics tools to understand which features are most used, identifying usability issues, or tailoring the user experience to different user groups.
  • •Marketing and Optional Communications: With your consent (for example, if you opt in to our newsletter or job alert emails), we will use your email or phone number to send marketing communications. These may include job newsletters, career advice, special offers, surveys, events, or other promotional information related to employment and recruitment. You can opt out of marketing communications at any time, and opting out will not affect your core use of the service.
  • •Payment Processing and Invoicing: For business users who purchase paid services, we use personal and company information to process transactions, issue invoices, send payment receipts, and manage billing. This may involve sharing necessary information with third-party payment gateways as described in the Sharing section.
  • •Security and Fraud Prevention: We use personal data (such as device information, IP addresses, and usage logs) to monitor for suspicious or fraudulent activity and to enforce our Terms of Service. This includes verifying a user’s identity, investigating complaints or violations, and taking measures to maintain the integrity and security of the platform.
  • •Legal Compliance: We may process and retain personal information as needed to comply with applicable laws and regulations. For instance, we might keep transaction records to satisfy financial reporting laws or use personal information to respond to lawful requests by public authorities (such as law enforcement or court orders). We also process personal data to exercise or defend legal claims, if necessary.
  • •Service Development and Research: We may use data in an aggregated or anonymized form (which does not identify individual users) to understand employment trends, to perform statistical analyses, or to develop new features. For example, analyzing aggregated user data can help us improve our job matching algorithms or gain insights into job market demand. These analytics will not identify you personally.
  • •Outsourced Development and Operations: Because the Jabchaja platform was developed and is maintained with the assistance of an outsourced third-party developer, we may use personal information in collaboration with that third party strictly for the purpose of operating, troubleshooting, and improving the service. (More details are provided in the “Sharing of Information” section below.) The outsourced development team follows our instructions on how to handle personal data and is bound by confidentiality and data protection obligations.
  • •We will not use personal information for purposes other than those stated above without obtaining your consent, and we do not engage in automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal effects concerning users without human intervention (except for basic automated matching or filtering functionalities inherent in a job platform). If we need to use your data for a new purpose, we will provide you with notice and seek your consent when required.

5. Sharing of Personal Information

  • •We do not sell or rent your personal information to third parties. However, in order to operate our service and fulfill the purposes described, we do share or entrust personal information in the following situations
  • •With Other Users (Recruitment Process): The primary instance of data sharing is between job seekers and business recruiters through the platform. If you are an individual job seeker, your profile information and application details will be shared with the specific employer/recruiter you choose to apply to or communicate with on Jabchaja. Recruiters receiving your data are required to use it only for recruitment purposes (e.g., evaluating your application and contacting you) and to protect it in accordance with law, but they are independent data controllers of that information once received. If you make your resume public in our database (a feature which is optional and subject to your consent), it may be viewable by registered business recruiters searching for candidates. If you are a business user, certain business-related information (such as your company name, industry, and job posting content, and possibly the recruiter’s name or work contact info you provide in job listings) will be visible to job seekers using the platform. This sharing is inherent to the Jabchaja service functionality.
  • •Service Providers and Outsourced Processing: We employ trusted third-party companies and individuals to support our services (commonly referred to as “data processors” or service providers). This includes our outsourced development partner that helped build the Jabchaja platform and continues to assist with its technical maintenance and improvement. It may also include web hosting providers, cloud infrastructure services, email delivery services, analytics providers, customer support tools, and payment processors. These third parties process personal information on our behalf only for the purposes of providing their services to us. We share with them only the minimum data necessary (for example, our cloud hosting provider stores the databases that contain user information; our email service is given users’ email addresses to send notification emails; our payment processor receives billing information to process transactions). All service providers are bound by contracts that enforce data protection obligations, require them to keep information confidential, and prohibit them from using your information for any unrelated purposes.
  • •Third-Party Partners With Your Consent: Aside from the core service processes, we will only share your personal information with third parties for secondary or marketing purposes if you have given explicit consent. For example, if in the future we run a joint event or promotion with a partner and you opt in to have your information shared, or you agree to receive job recommendations from a partner site, we would inform you and seek your consent for such sharing. You will have the choice not to participate.
  • •Legal and Compliance: We may disclose personal information to third parties if we believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to: comply with a legal obligation or request (for example, to respond to subpoenas, court orders, or government regulations), enforce our Terms of Service or other agreements, investigate and defend the Company against legal claims or allegations, protect the rights, property, or safety of the Company, our users, or the public. This includes exchanging information with law enforcement agencies or regulators when required by law.
  • •Business Transfers: If the Company undergoes a business transaction such as a merger, acquisition by another company, reorganization, or sale of all or part of its assets, personal information we hold may be transferred to the successor or acquiring entity. In such an event, we will ensure that the new owner will continue to be bound by the terms of this Privacy Policy or will provide notice and obtain consent if required for any changes in data processing. We will notify users (for example, via email or a notice on our site) of any such change in ownership or control of personal information.
  • •Aggregated or De-Identified Data: We may share aggregated, anonymized statistical data with third parties (such as number of users in a region, popular job categories, etc.) for industry analysis, research, or marketing. This data will not identify any individual and thus is not considered personal information.

6. User Rights and Choices

  • •We recognize that individuals have rights regarding their personal data. In accordance with Korean privacy law and global data protection principles, you have the following rights with respect to the personal information we hold about you
  • •Right to Access: You have the right to request confirmation of whether we are processing your personal data, and to access or receive a copy of the personal information we hold about you. For example, job seekers can view and edit their profile data anytime by logging into their account. If you need a complete export of your data, you can request it from us.
  • •Right to Rectification: If any of your personal information is inaccurate or outdated, you have the right to request correction or update of that data. You can correct most information through your account settings (e.g., update your contact info or resume). For any details you cannot change yourself, you can contact us and we will make the correction if appropriate.
  • •Right to Deletion: You have the right to request deletion (erasure) of your personal data that we hold. You can achieve this in many cases by deleting your account through the platform’s account settings (for both individual and business users, there is an account termination option). Upon your request, we will delete or anonymize your personal information, except for information we are required to retain by law or for legitimate business purposes as outlined in the retention section. If your personal data has been provided to third parties (such as a resume sent to an employer via an application), we cannot directly delete the data held by that third party, but we can remove it from our own systems and inform you how you may contact the third party.
  • •Right to Withdraw Consent: Where we rely on your consent to process information (for example, for receiving marketing emails or for making a resume public), you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal will not affect the lawfulness of any processing we already performed based on your consent in the past. You can opt out of marketing communications by using the unsubscribe link in emails or adjusting your account preferences. To withdraw consent for any optional data collection, you may adjust settings or contact us.
  • •Right to Restrict or Object (Data Processing Restrictions): You have the right, in certain situations, to request that we restrict the processing of your personal information or to object to specific processing activities. For instance, if you contest the accuracy of your data, you can request we pause processing until verification; or you might object to processing that you feel is not justified. We will honor such requests where required by applicable law. In particular, if you are in a jurisdiction like the EU (GDPR applicable), you may object to processing based on our legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • •Right to Data Portability: To the extent applicable (for example, under GDPR for EU users), you can request a copy of certain personal information in a machine-readable format so that you can transfer it to another service. For example, you might request an export of your profile and resume data. We will provide this where technically feasible and as required by law.
  • •Right to Not Be Subject to Automated Decisions: Jabchaja does not make any legally significant decisions about individuals purely by algorithms without human involvement (like automated rejections without recruiter review). If that policy changes, affected users would have the right to request human intervention or to contest the decision.
  • •Right to Lodge a Complaint: If you believe that we have handled your personal information improperly or in violation of your rights, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a relevant supervisory authority or regulatory body in your jurisdiction. In Korea, you can contact the Personal Information Protection Commission (PIPC) or the Korea Internet & Security Agency (KISA)’s Personal Data Infringement Report Center. For users in other regions (e.g., European Union residents), you may lodge a complaint with your local Data Protection Authority. We encourage you, however, to first reach out to us so we can address your concerns directly.
  • •Exercising Your Rights: Most of the rights above can be exercised directly by logging into your Jabchaja account and using the profile or account settings (for example, editing your information or deleting your account). If you need any assistance or wish to make a formal request, you may contact our Data Protection Officer (see Contact section below). For security, we may need to verify your identity before fulfilling certain requests (such as access or deletion requests) to ensure that we do not disclose or remove information improperly. Verification might involve confirming information we already have on file or asking for identification. We will respond to legitimate requests within the timeframe required by law (generally within 30 days in many jurisdictions). There is no fee for making such requests, unless they are excessively repetitive or manifestly unfounded, in which case we will inform you of any cost before proceeding.
  • •Please note that in some cases, we may have lawful grounds to refuse certain requests. For example, if you request deletion of data which we are mandated by law to keep, or if fulfilling your request would infringe on another person’s rights or would cause the inability to deliver our services (e.g., deleting all usage records might interfere with fraud prevention). If we deny your request, we will explain the reasons to the extent permitted. We remain committed to upholding your rights and ensuring you have full control over your personal information.

7. Data Security Measures

  • •We take the security of your personal information very seriously. The Company has implemented a variety of technical, administrative, and physical security measures to protect your data from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include, but are not limited to, the following
  • •Encryption: We use encryption protocols to protect personal data. All communications between your browser/app and our servers are secured via SSL/TLS encryption (HTTPS), which protects data in transit from eavesdropping. Sensitive information (such as passwords) is stored in encrypted or hashed form in our database.
  • •Access Controls: We limit access to personal information to authorized personnel who need the data to perform their job duties. Each such employee or contractor is bound by confidentiality obligations. We implement role-based access controls and authentication mechanisms to ensure that staff can only access the data necessary for their role. Administrative access to systems containing personal data is logged and monitored.
  • •Security Testing and Monitoring: Our platform undergoes regular security assessments. We use firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and anti-malware tools to guard against external attacks. We keep our software and infrastructure updated with the latest security patches. Periodic vulnerability scans and penetration tests are conducted (either by our internal team or external security experts) to identify and address potential security weaknesses.
  • •Data Minimization and Pseudonymization: Wherever feasible, we minimize the use of personal identifiers and use pseudonymization. For example, user passwords are never stored in plain text but hashed; certain identifiers may be tokenized. We collect only the personal information that is necessary for each purpose, which reduces the risk exposure in case of any security issue.
  • •Physical Security: The servers and data centers where personal information is stored are secured facilities with access control (e.g., key card or biometric entry, security personnel, CCTV surveillance). We either use reputable third-party hosting providers with strong security certifications or maintain secure on-premise servers. Backup media and paper records (if any) are kept in locked, access-controlled environments.
  • •Employee Training and Policies: We have internal policies for data protection and train our employees about their responsibilities in handling personal information. Employees are educated on privacy best practices, and we conduct background checks on staff who handle sensitive data when permitted by law. In addition, we have incident response procedures in place to handle any suspected breach of personal data swiftly and effectively.
  • •Audit and Compliance: We maintain logs of access to personal data and regularly review these logs for any unauthorized activity. Our security practices are aligned with industry standards and we may undergo audits or certifications as required. We also require our third-party service providers to implement robust security measures; they must comply with applicable security standards and are subject to audits and assessments.
  • •Despite our best efforts, please note that no method of transmitting or storing data is completely secure. While we strive to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security. You are also responsible for keeping your account credentials (passwords, one-time verification codes, etc.) confidential and for notifying us immediately if you suspect any unauthorized use of your account. In the unfortunate event of a data breach that affects your personal information, we will notify you and the appropriate authorities as required by law, and we will take all necessary steps to mitigate the impact and prevent future occurrences.

8. International Data Transfers

  • •Given the global nature of internet services and modern cloud infrastructure, the personal information you provide to Jabchaja may be transferred to, stored in, or processed in a country other than your own. In particular, if you are using our service from a country outside of where our servers or offices are located, your data will necessarily cross international borders.
  • •Primary Data Location: Our main operations are based in the Republic of Korea, and generally, user data is stored on servers located in Korea. However, we may utilize cloud services or technical support located in other jurisdictions. For example, our outsourced development team or certain service providers might operate from or use servers in countries such as the United States, European Union member states, or other locations.
  • •Risks and Safeguards: Different countries have different data protection laws, some of which may not be as strict as those in your home jurisdiction. Regardless of location, we will always safeguard your information as described in this Privacy Policy. When we transfer personal data out of its country of origin, we take steps to ensure appropriate protections are in place. These steps may include
  • •Standard Contractual Clauses: If data from users in jurisdictions like the European Economic Area (EEA) is transferred to a country not deemed adequate by the EU, we implement European Commission-approved Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) in our contracts with the data importer to ensure they will protect the data according to EU standards.
  • •Korean Data Export Compliance: For transfers out of Korea, we comply with relevant provisions of the Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA), which may include obtaining your consent for overseas transfer of certain personal information when required, or ensuring the recipient country or organization has an equivalent level of data protection.
  • •Other Safeguards: We may rely on additional safeguards such as obtaining certifications or assurances from the receiving party (for example, verifying if the recipient is certified under frameworks like the APEC Cross-Border Privacy Rules system, if applicable), and conducting risk assessments for cross-border data transfers. Our service providers who handle or store data in other countries are all contractually bound to protect your information to high standards.
  • •Our email notification service might route communications through servers outside your country for efficiency.
  • •If our development or customer support team is in another country, they might access user data remotely to fix technical issues or assist you, under strict controls and only as needed.
  • •Backup data may be stored in cloud storage which could involve data centers in multiple regions for redundancy.
  • •Your Choices: By using the Jabchaja platform and providing your information, you acknowledge that your personal information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own. We understand that international transfers can pose additional risks to data protection, and we mitigate those risks as described. If you prefer not to have your data transferred outside your country, please contact us to discuss your concerns. However, please note that in many cases, we may need to transfer data internationally to provide the service (for example, if our servers or service providers are based overseas). If you cannot consent to such international transfer, it may be difficult for us to offer you our services.
  • •If we transfer your personal data to new categories of recipients or new countries not covered by this Privacy Policy, we will update this Policy and notify users as required, obtaining consent where necessary. Our goal is to ensure that your personal information enjoys the same level of protection no matter where it is handled.
